>Chow Mein Recipe

Chow mein is a popular Chinese dish that has become a staple in many Asian cuisines. The dish consists of stir-fried noodles that are typically made from wheat flour, eggs, and water, along with various vegetables, meat, and seafood.

The noodles are cooked in a wok or large frying pan over high heat, along with the other ingredients, which are stir-fried until they are tender and infused with the flavors of the sauce. Chow mein is a versatile dish that can be customized to suit individual tastes and preferences, and it is often served as a main course or a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz chow mein noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as sliced carrots, bell peppers, and bok choy)
  • 1 cup cooked meat (such as sliced chicken or beef)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step by Step

  1. Cook the chow mein noodles according to package instructions until they are al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over high heat. Add the onion and garlic and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they are slightly softened.
  4. Add the cooked meat to the pan and stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  5. Add the cooked chow mein noodles to the pan along with the soy sauce and oyster sauce. Toss everything together until the noodles and vegetables are evenly coated with the sauce.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste, and serve hot.
