Shumai Recipe

Shumai, also known as siu mai or shaomai, is a type of Chinese dumpling that is typically served as a dim sum dish. The dumplings are made with a thin wrapper made from wheat or rice flour that is filled with a mixture of ground pork, shrimp, and seasonings such as ginger and soy sauce. The dumplings are typically steamed until they are tender and the filling is cooked through. Shumai is a popular snack or appetizer in Chinese cuisine, and it is often served with soy sauce or chili oil for dipping.

Shumai is a versatile dish that can be customized with different fillings and seasonings to suit individual tastes and preferences. Vegetarian shumai, made with fillings such as mushrooms and tofu, is also a popular option. The dumplings are easy to make at home, and they are a great way to impress guests at a party or to enjoy as a snack or light meal.

Ingredients for Shumai:

  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or dry sherry
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 24-30 shumai wrappers
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, shrimp, soy sauce, rice wine or sherry, sesame oil, cornstarch, ginger, garlic, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to combine.
  2. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each shumai wrapper.
  3. Gather the edges of the wrapper around the filling, pinching them together at the top to create a pleated effect.
  4. Place the shumai in a steamer basket, leaving a little bit of space between each dumpling.
  5. Steam the shumai for 8-10 minutes until the filling is cooked through and the wrappers are tender.
  6. Serve hot with soy sauce or chili oil for dipping.
